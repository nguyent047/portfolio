Attractive Cities

Haarlem

If you are keen for a change of scenery, you can be in Haarlem by train within fifteen minutes. Visit the Frans Hals museum (Frans Hals is a famous Dutch painter), have a drink at the Grote Markt or go shopping in the charming old town centre.

Alkmaar

Alkmaar, the Dutch cheese town, is only half an hour by train. From April to October, each Friday morning there is the famous traditional cheese market.

Utrecht

Utrecht’s city centre, reachable within half an hour by train, has retained more of its medieval origin. The Cathedral Tower (de Dom) in the old town is the most famous monument in Utrecht. It is possible to climb the Dom Tower and enjoy an excellent panoramic view of the city and its surroundings. In the Utrecht Museum Quarter you can walk from one museum to the next, such as the Centraal Museum and the Nederlands Spoorwegmuseum (Dutch Railway Museum). The heart of Utrecht also offers you a huge shopping mall, Hoog Catherijne. You walk straight into it when you exit the train station.

Rotterdam

Remember to visit Rotterdam, the port city. You can get there by train in an hour. There are many museums, theatres, clubs, monuments and shops. In Rotterdam you can visit the large harbour, the Erasmus Bridge, the Euromast, the Diergaarde Blijdorp Zoo and many other highlights.

Strand Zuid

In the RAI area, next to Beatrixpark, you can visit a city beach of 2,000 square metres with deck chairs, an outdoor café and you can even get there by boat if you prefer.

Den Haag

Although Amsterdam is the capital of the Netherlands, the seat of government is in the seaside town of Den Haag (The Hague). Apart from the beach you can visit the houses of parliament and the wonderful museumHetMauritshuis,whichoffers state-of-the-art exibitions of paintings. Den Haag’s municipal museum (gemeentemuseum) is also very good. A lot of tourists like visiting Madurodam, a park featuring Dutch highlights in miniature.
