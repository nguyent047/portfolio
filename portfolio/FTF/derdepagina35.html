International Student Organisations

Ep-Nuffic

The major organisation providing support to foreign students is the Organisation for Internationalisation in Education (Ep-Nuffic). Ep-Nuffic (www.epnuffic.nl/en/) provides foreign students with information about international courses available in the Netherlands:

  • The Dutch system of higher education
  • The practical, day-to-day aspects of living in the Netherlands, including information about residence permits
  • Relevant news articles and comments on the Netherlands via a newsletter which is distributed every two weeks.

ISN Amsterdam

International Student Network (ISN) Amsterdam is a student-run organisation dedicated to help international students studying at the UvA and the HvA in making the most of their stay in the Netherlands. ISN is part of the Erasmus Student Network (ESN), an organisation with 505 local sections in 38 countries. They are here to ensure that you are going to have the time of your life studying abroad. ISN achieves this by organising great introduction days, parties, weekend trips, excursions and other cultural activities. This dynamic organisation promotes integration through the Dutch coaches who guide international students during the introduction period, as well as a sophisticated buddy system. Some classic examples of ISN activities include the Discover Holland weekend, Dutch movie nights, Dutch lessons and daytrips to the places in the Netherlands you must not miss. In addition to this, ISN even organises a number of international trips to popular European destinations such as Prague, Paris and London. With an ESN card you can join all the ISN activities. In addition to this, this card provides you with various great discounts at their weekly borrel (drinks) and lots of museums, shops and restaurants. Check out www.isn-amsterdam.nl or nd ISN on Facebook to stay updated!

ESN VU Amsterdam

ESN VU Amsterdam is the international student association of VU University Amsterdam. The mission of ESN is to contribute to the experience of exchange students in Amsterdam by organising social events and establishing links with organisations in order to bridge the gap between students and rms. At the beginning of each semester, ESN organises the ISI Days (International Student Introduction Days), an introduction to Amsterdam. Other events can be weekly borrels (drinks), hitchhiking weekends, bowling nights and ice-skating trips, or even city trips and full- edged weekends into the great outdoors (in the Netherlands or abroad). Stay updated through Facebook and www.esnvuamsterdam.nl.

ESN Inholland Diemen

ESN Diemen aims to help all incoming international and exchange students to socially integrate at Inholland Diemen and therefore also in Dutch society. ESN InHolland Diemen does this by organising activities and workshops on both an educational and socio-cultural level. For more information check the website www.esndiemen.nl.
