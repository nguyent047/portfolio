Places to live

It is not easy to find a place to live in Amsterdam. That's why it's a good idea to view all of the options first.

Student housing

To find a room in Amsterdam, international students can contact their host university. Most universities offer special housing services for international students.

Private renting

Visit this page to find more information about private renting and how to do so.

Housing corporations

There are three major housing corporations, De Key, DUWO and Ymere. These have one central information desk for student housing while the application process involves a waiting-list system.

Commercial Agency

Another option is going to a commercial agency. On this page you'll there will be information on where uou could exactly go.

Temporary accommodation

If you can't find any place to stay and yet you'll really need one, you should find a temporary accommodation first.

Student jobs

Life in Amsterdam is not cheap. The obvious solution is to combine your studies with a job. Although the city offers enough jobs, foreign students can experience some obstacles. The tips offered to you here should help you get around them.

Money Matters

The Dutch currency is the euro. This European currency is valid in most EU countries. The currency is decimal; one euro is equivalent to hundred euro cents.
