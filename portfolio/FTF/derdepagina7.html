Bank Holidays

Kerstmis (Christmas)

Most people celebrate Christmas with their family by having a Christmas tree and a luxurious dinner. A lot of churches have special Christmas services. In recent years more and more people have adopted the Anglo-American tradition of exchanging Christmas presents, making the Kerstman (Santa Claus) a friendly rival to Sinterklaas. However, not many people know that the Kerstman was based on Sinterklaas. Christmas is celebrated on 25 and 26 December.

Nieuwjaarsdag (New Year's Day)

OnNewYear’sDaymanyDutch people visit their relatives to wish them a Happy New Year. Some people traditionally take an icy dip in the North Sea (de Nieuwjaarsduik) to wash away the worries of the past year. This event is organised in several locations, the most popular of which is Scheveningen. In Amsterdam you can go to the Sloterplas for a dip. See www.nieuwjaarsduik.info for more information (Dutch only).

Goede Vrijdag (Good Friday)

Good Friday is the Friday before Easter. On this day Christians commemorate the cruci xion and death of Jesus in Jerusalem. In 2017 it will be on 14 April.

Easter

Easter will be celebrated on 16 and 17 April 2017 and is usually spent with relatives. During these days Christians celebrate the resurrection of Christ. Others enjoy having time off, searching for and eating eggs and chocolate.

Koningsdag (King's Day)

Until 2014 it was Queen’s day the Dutch celebrated yearly on 30 April. After the current King Willem Alexander’s succession to the throne it is now King’s Day they celebrate on his birthday, 27 April. The King and members of the Royal Family pay a public visit to one or two cities. All over the country, people participate in street markets much like yard sales and perform or play games in the streets. There are food and drink stalls and concerts in every town in the Netherlands. Most people wear orange, the royal colour. The night before King’s Day, Koningsnacht, almost everybody goes out. There are many special parties, although you will have to buy tickets in advance because usually these are in high demand.

Hemelvaartsdag (Ascention day)

This Christian holiday marks Jesus’ ascension to heaven. Ascension Day always falls on a Thursday. In 2017, it will be on 25 May, ten days before Pentecost.

Pinksteren (Pentecost)

Pentecost is the ftieth day after Easter. In 2017 Pentecost is on 4 and 5 June. This day commemorates the descent of the Holy Spirit. Regarding Christian holidays, not all Dutch people know what these involve. However, they are extremely grateful for the days off.
