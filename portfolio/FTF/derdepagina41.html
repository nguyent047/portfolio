Sexuality

Sexual Harassment

Each university and college has appointed a vertrouwenspersoon (sexual harassment officer) for victims of sexual harassment. This is someone to whom victims can turn for help and report their case in confidence. Check your university website for further information.

Renting

If you do not need a bike regularly, you can also rent one. In the address section we have listed a couple of places. You will have to pay a deposit to take a bike out, but some shops accept your passport and credit card instead of cash. The cheapest day rate is around €9.50; the deposit can be anything from €50, a passport, identity papers or credit card. Many Dutch bikes brake by back-pedalling. This can take some time to get used to, so if you prefer handbrakes, ask for a bike with hand- brakes. Students can also rent a bike for a special student rate at Rent-a-bike.

AIDS and STD

If you have any questions about HIV or AIDS, you can call the free AIDS Information Line on 020 689 25 77. To get tested for HIV or Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STD) go to the GGD. Testing there isanonymousandfree. Consultation hours: Monday to Friday 08.30 to 10.00 hrs and 13.30 to 16.30 hrs (for tests), Monday to Friday 08.30 to 12.00 hrs and 13.30 to 17.00 hrs (results and treatments). You can also get a test from your general practitioner. For more information about AIDS and other STDs, call the GG and GD on 020 555 58 22. For more information about aids and sexuality, check out these websites: www.soaaids.nl or www.aids.org.
